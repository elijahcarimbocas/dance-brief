03 Where I keep getting stuck

Four specific things. The video appendix (§04) is timestamped to each.

3.1 Adapting to the follow's level mid-dance

In every social dance I have to size up the follow and adjust my moveset on the fly. Some follows can't match the level a move needs, so in the moment I retreat to simpler, predictable patterns - and I default all the way back to the moves from my first Fast-Track Combo. That kills flow state and groove. What I want is the neuroplasticity to have a wider set of reads and outs, so I can probe a follow's level with mid-range moves instead of collapsing to my safest ones.

Flow stateReading the followMove vocabularyDefault regression
3.2 Salsa footwork on specific figures

Most of salsa is cross-body transitions and my cross-body footwork is solid. Where I miss is figures that need a rockstep / cumbia step instead - for example cross-body double-handed switch-hand turns, or when the lead has the follow in a switch-hand grip and places himself into a hammerlock. Those have their own footwork and I lose it.

RockstepCumbia stepSwitch-hand turnsHammerlock
3.3 Clarity vs. aggression on demanding combos

On more demanding combos there's a fine line between being clear to the follow and being too aggressive with the lead. I always err toward clarity/softness because I've heard follows complain about over-aggressive leads. I want to know where the actual line is so my leads stay clean and readable at higher difficulty without going soft.

Lead clarityConnection / frameTone of lead
3.4 Advanced hand toss techniques

Several combos use advanced hand tosses - behind-the-back, simultaneous-with-a-rockstep, and toss-into-turn. I understand the idea but I'm still fighting the execution and timing. Videos below show the specific ones.
